Who Are Kneecap

Kneecap are a rap group from Northern Ireland, rapping in Irish and English. They mix politics, humour, and youth culture in a bold, chaotic style. Their music is unapologetically loud, smart, and local.

Origin Story

They started to make music to express themselves, show local culture, and make Irish relevant to young people. They wanted something fresh and unapologetic, reflecting real-life experiences.

Style & Vibe

The music mixes chaotic beats, witty lyrics, and energetic performance. It’s aggressive, playful, and always meaningful, with a strong Irish identity.

Name Meaning

“Kneecap” is memorable, bold, and slightly shocking. It reflects their rebellious style, humour, and intention to stand out.

Why They Matter

Kneecap makes Irish rap accessible and culturally relevant, giving a voice to youth and challenging mainstream expectations.

Móglaí Bap

Pronounced MOH glee bap

Age / County: Around 30, Belfast

Hobbies: Writing lyrics, reading Irish literature, football, debating politics

How They Started: Started rapping with friends in Belfast to make Irish modern and relevant

Role: Main lyricist, clever wordplay, balances humour and serious commentary

Fun Fact: Often includes local Belfast slang that only locals fully understand

Mo Chara

Pronounced MOH KHAR uh

Age / County: 26-28, Belfast

Hobbies: Skateboarding, performing live, social commentary, gaming

How They Started: Performing to channel frustration about youth issues and inequality

Role: Emotional powerhouse, adds raw energy and intensity

Fun Fact: Improvises parts of live performances to hype the crowd

DJ Próvaí

Pronounced DEE JAY PROH vee

Age / County: 34-35, Derry

Hobbies: Producing beats, DJing, exploring music genres, coding music software

How They Started: Producing to create a unique sound and bring Irish beats into hip hop

Fun Fact: Sometimes mixes traditional Irish instruments into modern trap beats
